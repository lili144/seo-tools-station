How to Check Keyword Density — A Beginner's Guide

June 26, 2025·5 min read

Keyword density is the percentage of times a keyword appears on a page compared to the total word count. While Google has never published an official guideline, most SEO experts agree that 1% to 3% is the healthy range. That means your primary keyword should appear roughly 1–3 times per 100 words.

Anything above 5% starts to look like keyword stuffing — a practice Google penalized years ago with the Panda update. On the flip side, below 0.5% might not give search engines enough signal about your topic. The key is balance: your content should read naturally to humans while giving search engines clear topical signals.

Why Keyword Density Still Matters in 2025

Some SEOs claim keyword density is dead. That's only half true. While Google now uses sophisticated natural language processing (NLP) to understand content beyond exact-match keywords, keyword frequency still influences topical relevance. Pages that mention a target keyword in the right places — title, H1, first paragraph, and naturally throughout the body — consistently outperform pages that never mention it at all.

More importantly, checking your keyword density helps you catch two common problems: over-optimization (you're repeating the same phrase so much it sounds robotic) and under-optimization (your content is on the right topic but uses none of the terms your audience searches for).

How to Check Your Density: Step by Step

  1. Copy your content — the full text of your article, blog post, or landing page.
  2. Paste it into a keyword density checker — our free tool analyzes every word, filters out stop words (the, and, is, etc.), and shows frequency percentages for all meaningful terms.
  3. Review your primary keyword — is it in the 1–3% range? Check that it also appears in your title, first paragraph, and at least one H2 subheading.
  4. Identify unintentional repetition — you might be surprised to find you've used "best" or "really" 20 times without realizing it.
  5. Check for related terms — does your content include synonyms and semantically related keywords? Google uses LSI to understand context, not just exact matches.

Placement Beats Density Every Time

Where you put your keyword matters more than how many times you use it. Ensure your primary keyword appears in these seven critical locations:

  • ✅ Title tag (as close to the beginning as possible)
  • ✅ Meta description (naturally, not forced)
  • ✅ URL slug (e.g., /blog/check-keyword-density/)
  • ✅ H1 heading
  • ✅ First 100 words of the body
  • ✅ At least one H2 subheading
  • ✅ Image alt text (where relevant)

Common Mistakes

Keyword stuffing is obvious — repeating your keyword until the text is unreadable. But the opposite mistake is just as common: writing content that never mentions the target keyword, hoping Google will "figure it out." Google is smart, but it still relies on textual signals.

Another trap is over-focusing on one exact phrase. A post about "best running shoes" should also discuss "athletic footwear," "jogging sneakers," "marathon training gear," and "trail running." This builds topical depth and helps you rank for dozens of related queries instead of just one.

The Bottom Line

Write for humans first, then use a density checker to fine-tune. If your content sounds natural when read aloud and your primary keyword appears in all the right places at 1–3% density, you're in good shape. Google rewards content that serves user intent — and that's what ultimately drives rankings.
